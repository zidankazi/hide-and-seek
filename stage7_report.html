The reversal

For ten runs, one hider learned to do exactly one thing: run. A lone seeker moves at the same speed, so evasion always wins and no tool ever pays for itself — a full 40-million-step co-evolution ended with the hider hidden 98% of the play phase and neither side ever touching the ramp. ↑The arms race

With two seekers the game becomes a genuine arms race. Over 40M steps the seekers learn to fetch and stand on the ramp, and every point of ramp-use chips away at the hider's ability to disappear. This crossing curve is the emergence we were hunting — earned on the seeker side.

✕Why the first rung looked like a wall

For a long time rung 1 never came — and it isn't for lack of reward. But the failure wasn't really acquisition; it only looked that way. The deeper cause: in the roomy map the hider already wins 78% of the time by plain hiding, so the barricade is a luxury the reward never demands. Bigger batches, memory, and 120M steps all left it at 0.0% — not because the skill is unlearnable, but because nothing made it necessary. (Shrink the room and it emerges — see the verdict.)

The bug that rewrote the earlier verdict

"Needs industrial compute" was partly a measurement artifact

Earlier stages concluded emergence simply needed more scale. But the exploration noise had a constant upward pressure and no ceiling, so the longer a run trained, the more its policy decayed into pure randomness. The one early stage that did show tool-use had the lowest noise of all; the 10-million-step runs had drifted to a sampling spread of ~1013 — every action a coin flip.

Every verdict built on long runs was contaminated by noise, not limited by compute. Clamping it was fix #1 of eleven.

Thirteen walls, one at a time

Each fix removed exactly one blocker and exposed the next — the debugging was the research. Every metric below is a 200-episode deterministic eval with no assists.

  1. Exploration runaway
    Clamp the policy's noise; the entropy bonus was ratcheting it to infinity.
  2. Doorway plug
    A spawn helper was passively hiding the hider, so the seeker never learned to hunt.
  3. Ramp discovery
    Start seekers on the ramp sometimes — this ignited rung 2.
  4. Distance-evasion
    Widen elevated sight so dodging inside the room stops beating the ramp.
  5. Frozen opponents
    A stale opponent pool let the seeker unlearn the ramp; go to a live ladder.
  6. Lock thrashing
    Noise kept toggling a just-placed lock off; make locking idempotent.
  7. Mean vs noise
    Clipped actions made the mean policy irrelevant; cap the spread so it matters.
  8. Reverse-chained curriculum
    Start episodes one action from a rung's payoff so its value is learnable.
  9. Capacity
    A wider fresh network learned in 3.5M what the legacy one couldn't in 48M.
  10. Multi-agent pressure
    The load-bearing one: two seekers break evasion and let tools pay off.
  11. Batch size
    4× the rollout — cut gradient noise to nothing, and confirmed rung 1 is a real wall.
  12. Recurrent memory
    The paper's architecture. Nearly doubled rung 2 (20.8%) — and left rung 1 at 0.0%.
  13. Paper-regime scale
    120M steps, the last untried lever. Rung 1 held at 0.0% the whole way — scale ruled out.
Verdict

A tool emerges only when the environment makes it necessary — never because there's more compute.

One seeker is dodgeable and nothing emerges at any compute budget. Two seekers make evasion unwinnable, and seeker ramp-use appears from scratch. Construction — the hider barricading the door — looked like a hard wall: 0.0% across batch size, recurrent memory, and 120M steps of paper-regime scale. But the counterfactual gave it away: 78% hidden from room geometry alone. The barricade was never learned because it was never needed — plain hiding already won. Shrink the room until evasion fails, and the same policy that never barricaded in 120M steps builds a locked barricade in ~22% of episodes, unassisted. Every rung is gated by necessity, not compute: make the tool the only way to win, and it emerges.

METHOD — PettingZoo + pymunk, custom PPO, 128–256-unit MLPs. Line-of-sight reward only; no reward shaping. Numbers are 200-episode deterministic evaluations with zero training assists. "1 seeker" columns are the same policies re-evaluated against a single seeker — the control isolating pressure from policy.

The paper's recipe — recurrent memory and 100M+ steps — was tested directly and left construction at 0.0%. What finally moved it was neither: a tighter room where a seeker at the doorway sees everything, so hiding fails and sealing the door is the only refuge. Rung 1 then emerges and holds at ~20–25% (honest, no assists). The mirror image also holds — the tight room de-emphasizes the ramp, so rung 2 fades there. Each rung emerges in the environment that makes it necessary; no single tuning makes the paper's full three-rung chain co-emerge at this scale. Full run-by-run journal in NOTES.md.
